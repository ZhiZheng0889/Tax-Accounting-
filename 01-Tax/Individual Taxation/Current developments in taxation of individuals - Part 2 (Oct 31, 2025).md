# Current developments in taxation of individuals: Part 2 (Oct. 31, 2025)

**Type:** Study guide (summary + practice)
**Topic:** Individual Income Taxation (recent federal developments)
**Coverage window:** Six months ending April 2025 (per article)
**Primary use:** Quick recall + exam/interview prompts + client-facing planning notes

## What this covers (high level)

This guide summarizes and drills the key takeaways from a semiannual update on individual federal tax developments, organized roughly by Code section. Focus is on what changed (cases/rulings/guidance), what the rule is, what the IRS/courts emphasized, and what you should do differently in practice.

## Learning objectives

After working this guide, you should be able to:

- Explain the substantiation theme running through the NOL cases (Sec. 172).
- Apply the Sec. 183 “hobby loss” framework using the nine nonexclusive regulatory factors.
- Distinguish deductible medical expenses from nondeductible personal/family arrangement costs (Sec. 213).
- State when moving expenses remain deductible post-TCJA (Sec. 217(g)).
- Describe select retirement-plan and inherited IRA developments affecting individuals (Sec. 401).
- Explain how passive activity credit limits can disallow investment credits (Sec. 469).
- Recall how Sec. 1256 interacts with Sec. 165(c) and profit motive limitations.
- Identify recurring issues in self-employment tax characterization (Secs. 1401–1402).
- Outline when innocent spouse relief is allowed vs denied and why (Sec. 6015).

## Map of topics in this guide

- Sec. 172 — Net operating loss deduction (substantiation)
- Sec. 183 — Activities not engaged in for profit (hobby loss)
- Sec. 213 — Medical expenses (IVF vs surrogacy)
- Sec. 217 — Moving expenses (post-TCJA military-only exception)
- Sec. 401 — Retirement plan updates (Roth catch-up proposals; inherited IRA transfers)
- Sec. 469 — Passive activity credit limitations (investment tax credits)
- Sec. 1256 — Mark-to-market contracts and individual loss limits
- Secs. 1401–1402 — Self-employment tax (recharacterization; limited partner exception)
- Sec. 6015 — Innocent spouse relief (granted and denied cases)
